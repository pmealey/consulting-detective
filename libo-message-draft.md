I've been working on a project to generate small Sherlock Holmes, Consulting Detective-like cases.

[url]https://www.aureliansystems.io/consulting-detective/[/url]

Basically, I'm using AI (various Claude models) and a narrative generation engine I've created to generate a crime, then generate a casebook for the crime. There are definitely some quirks in the generated cases, but they are broadly (from my reading) coherent.

If anyone, particularly the old Consulting Detective games group, is interested, I'd appreciate it if you could take a look and give me some feedback. Do the mysteries work? Are they interesting to solve? Are there any contradictory elements, spoilers, etc.? Any ideas for feature I can add or improvements I can make? Even simple feedback like 'I tried a case and it made sense / didn't make sense' would be welcome!

I haven't figured out the endgame yet - there are questions and an optimal path to compare to, but it still all feels pretty clunky. Long term plans include adding in some experts that the player can consult who may or may not help, depending on the setting/case, and attempting to create a larger casebook that allows for more more freeform investigation.
